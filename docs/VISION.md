# Duʿā Companion Vision

Duʿā Companion is a calm, devotional-first place for daily adhkār and duʿā. The app helps a person return to remembered words at the right moments of the day without turning remembrance into a noisy productivity product.

## Purpose

The project exists to:

- Support a gentle rhythm of Morning Adhkār, Evening Adhkār, and Before Sleep remembrance.
- Provide occasion-based duʿā collections that can be opened when needed without creating pressure or streak mechanics.
- Keep Arabic, transliteration, English translation, virtues, grades, and references close to the reading experience.
- Make progress visible only where it serves the devotional routine.
- Preserve a blue-toned, uncluttered, spacious interface that feels calm and companion-like.

## Product principles

1. **Devotional-first** — the duʿā text and its meaning should always feel more important than UI chrome.
2. **Calm over gamified** — tracking exists to gently support daily habits, not to create anxiety.
3. **Blue-toned and soft** — visual language should stay within the current calm blue palette, with collection-specific accents used sparingly.
4. **Uncluttered by default** — avoid extra panels, badges, menus, filters, or widgets unless they directly help reading or remembering.
5. **Respect collection intent** — daily collections can track progress; reference and occasion collections should remain browse/read experiences.
6. **Local and portable** — progress lives in the browser and can be exported/imported through Backup & Restore.

## Current shape

The app is a static single-page web app with:

- A left navigation sidebar and mobile top bar.
- A home dashboard with a large banner, daily tracked cards, habit tracker cards, and occasion cards.
- Collection pages that reuse one dynamic collection view.
- A full-screen Focus Mode for reading one duʿā at a time.
- Local backup and restore of tracked progress only.

## Audience experience

A user should be able to open the app and immediately understand:

- What to recite today.
- Which collections are part of daily practice.
- Which collections are references for specific occasions.
- How to enter Focus Mode and read calmly.
- How to protect browser-local progress before clearing data or changing devices.
